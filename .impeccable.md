# Cebby — Design Context

> Synthesized 2026-04-26 from a structured product/design session and validated against the Oct/Nov 2024 user survey (26 responses).
> **This is the persistent reference for every design and product decision on Cebby. Read this before touching the UI.**

---

## What Cebby Is (and Isn't)

**Cebby is a calm, public-utility directory of Cebu tech events.** It aggregates from Luma, Eventbrite, Meetup, Facebook, and other sources, applies light editorial cleanup via a trusted-organizer whitelist, and presents a glanceable feed that lets people stop platform-hopping.

It is **not** an organizer toolkit, an RSVP system, a check-in platform, a community network, or a marketplace. The original Cebby tried to be those things; the current Cebby explicitly does not. Features in the codebase that serve those orphaned use cases are dormant — kept in the repo, not surfaced or developed.

The relationship to monetization is the **public-utility model**: hobby today (zero-cost to operate), with deliberate room for community sponsorship later (one banner slot, one digest sponsor line, an annual "support Cebby" link). No premium tiers, no native ads in the feed, no account paywalls. **Sponsorability is a trust-and-relationship outcome, not a feature-richness one.**

## Users

### Primary persona — Active Cebu tech attendee

Goes to 1+ events per month. Today they find events via Facebook (~90% of survey respondents) plus word-of-mouth from peers/colleagues. Frustrated by:

1. **"Too many platforms to check"** (top survey pain point, 8+ mentions). Events scatter across Luma, Eventbrite, Meetup, FB groups, Discord servers — checking each is exhausting.
2. **"Hard to find events that match my interests"** (also 8+ mentions). Generic event titles, vendor pitches mixed with real talks, no consistent tagging.
3. **Facebook's algorithm hides events until days before they happen** (direct quote from one respondent, but the pattern is widespread).

**What they want from Cebby**: a single feed where they can scan upcoming events weeks in advance, filter by what matters to them, and subscribe once so events flow into their calendar without further effort.

### Secondary persona — Connected lurker / former organizer

Used to be deep in the scene, attended frequently, possibly organized events. Now attends a handful of times per year. Wants to **stay tangentially in the loop without re-committing**. Opens Cebby occasionally; would love it to feel passive (calendar subscription, not weekly app-opening).

**Both personas want the same product surface.** Active attendees and lurkers differ only in attendance frequency, not in UX needs. Designing for the lurker is designing for the active attendee at lower volume.

### Job-to-be-done (one sentence)

> *Help me see all the Cebu tech events worth knowing about, weeks ahead, in one place, without me having to remember to look.*

### Marketing one-liner (lifted from a survey response)

> **"All Cebu tech events. Weeks ahead. All sources. No algorithm."**

This is the pitch. Use it on `/about`, in empty states, on the App Store description.

## Brand Personality

### Three words: **calm, tactile, local**

- **Calm** — the lurker can scan without effort. The active attendee can find the meetup tonight without sifting through "tech entrepreneurship summit" spam. Whitespace, clear hierarchy, no shouting copy, no fake urgency.
- **Tactile** — surfaces feel like physical things. Pills are pillows. Buttons are pressable. Cards float. The "game UI" energy comes from *form discipline* and *tactility*, not from animation or color.
- **Local** — Cebu-warm, not generic-international. The cursive lowercase `cebby` wordmark signals friendliness; the curated whitelist signals insider knowledge of the scene.

### Voice & tone

- **Lowercase wordmark, sentence-case headings, real-language copy.** No marketing-speak.
- **Honest about what Cebby is.** "via Luma" / "via Eventbrite" attribution is always visible — Cebby aggregates, it doesn't pretend to be the host.
- **Bisaya-friendly when natural, never forced.** No mandatory localization performances. If a venue name is "SM Seaside Skyhall," ship that — readers can read.
- **Empty states should sound like a friend, not a system.** Not "No saved events" but "No events saved yet. Tap the bookmark on any event to keep track."

### Emotional goal

When a user closes Cebby, they should feel:
> *"Good — now I know what's happening. I didn't waste time. I'm not going to miss the thing I care about."*

Not excited. Not FOMO'd. **Quietly informed.**

## Aesthetic Direction

### Soft-3D dimensional minimalism

Calm by *form discipline*, not by visual silence. Surfaces have real elevation — vertical gradients, inset highlights, multi-layer drop shadows — so the interface feels like a series of physical cards floating above a warm cream background. The "game UI" feeling lives in the **tactility of individual elements**, not in heavy 3D rendering or motion.

Every dimensional cue is **CSS-only**: gradients, box-shadows, transforms. No WebGL, no Three.js, no Spline embeds, no scroll-driven 3D.

### References

- **hapsay** (the Cebu sports complex booking app) — the calibration target for elevation, pill density, and color saturation
- **iOS 17+ widgets** — the calmer end of soft-3D
- **Linear, Plain, Vercel** — for restraint and hierarchy
- **Modern fitness apps** (Whoop, Oura) — for tactile information density without screaming

### Anti-references — what Cebby must NOT become

- **Eventbrite** — corporate, ad-heavy, dense
- **Facebook Events** — chaotic, signal-to-noise problem
- **Generic "neon hackathon" dev portal** — the unused half of the old Tailwind config
- **Webflow-template gradient mesh marketing pages**
- **Luma's heavy 3D** — explicitly named in survey as a pain point. *"One thing I hated luma is the 3D animations that usually slows down my pc."* This is direct user evidence.

### Theme

**Light only.** No dark mode. Reasons:
- Active attendees use the app in mixed contexts (commute, cafe, bed). Browser/OS auto-darken is acceptable.
- Doubling design surface for a hobby project is a maintenance trap.
- The canonical-directory references (Wikipedia, NYT archive, Hacker News, Luma) all default light.

## Design Principles

These are the load-bearing principles. When in doubt, return here.

### 1. **The design's job is to disappear.**
A directory has succeeded when the user stops noticing the chrome and just sees the events. Anything decorative that doesn't help find or trust the events is working against the product.

### 2. **Fast and interactive, despite the 3D.**
Soft-3D feel is achieved with cheap CSS primitives (gradients, box-shadows, transforms) — never with WebGL, scroll-driven 3D, or animation libraries that tax the device. Survey evidence: a respondent abandoned Luma because "3D animations slowed down my PC during RSVP." This must never happen with Cebby.

Concrete commitments:
- Press feedback is `transform: scale()` only (GPU compositor path)
- No infinite ambient animations (no `gradientBG`, `floatShape`, `glitch`, `shimmer`, `sparkle`)
- Tab navigation feels instant — prefer Astro page transitions / view transitions over heavy client-side routers
- Images use native `loading="lazy"`, intrinsic sizes, modern formats
- The PWA cache should make repeat visits feel zero-network
- First Contentful Paint <1s on 3G; interactions respond <100ms

### 3. **Build the billboard, not the venue.**
A billboard tells you where the party is. It doesn't sell tickets, take RSVPs, or check IDs. Cebby never owns the registration — it deep-links to the source platform (`RSVP on Luma →`). User-perceived "I RSVP'd through Cebby" comes from the *quality of the handoff*, not from owning the action.

### 4. **The whitelist is the moat.**
Trusted-organizer curation is what nobody can replicate by scraping the same sources. It encodes the operator's taste and scene knowledge. Maintaining the whitelist is the single most important non-design activity. Every design decision should make whitelist-driven content feel canonical.

### 5. **Tactility on, ambient motion off.**
Movement should always be a response to user action (press, tap, hover, scroll-into-view). Never ambient. A still interface looks more credible than a kinetic one. Animations on infinite loops are the single biggest tell of "amateur project" — not because amateurs use them, but because professionals removed them.

### 6. **Trust through transparency.**
Always show where an event came from (`via Luma`, `via Eventbrite`). Never hide aggregation behind a façade. Lurkers and active attendees both verify before they trust; transparency is what earns sponsorability later.

## Design Tokens (Visual Vocabulary)

The full reference implementation lives in `apps/pwa/src/pages/design-preview.astro`. Key recipes:

### Color

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Surface base | `#FAFAF9` (off-white/cream) | Page background |
| Surface elevated | `#FFFFFF` → `#FAFAF9` (vertical gradient) | Cards, dock, chips inactive |
| Accent | `#9333EA` (purple-600) | Active states, primary CTAs, wordmark |
| Accent gradient | `#C084FC` → `#7E22CE` (purple-400 → purple-700, vertical) | Active chips, primary buttons |
| Ambient warmth | Multi-radial: purple 6%, amber 5%, green 4% over surface base | Page background — barely perceptible, gives floating feel |

**Status pill semantics (use exactly):**
| Status | Gradient | Text | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|
| `pill-emerald` | `#ECFDF5` → `#A7F3D0` | `#047857` | Free, Confirmed, Available |
| `pill-amber` | `#FFFBEB` → `#FCD34D` | `#92400E` | Beginner-friendly, Pending |
| `pill-rose` | `#FFF1F2` → `#FDA4AF` | `#9F1239` | Paid, Sold out |
| `pill-blue` | `#EFF6FF` → `#93C5FD` | `#1E40AF` | Hiring, Featured |
| `pill-stone` | `#FAFAF9` → `#D6D3D1` | `#44403C` | Online, Neutral, Other |

### Typography

- **Body / UI**: Inter (400, 500, 600, 700, 800). System fallbacks for resilience.
- **Wordmark only**: Caveat 700. Used for `cebby` lockup. **Not for headings, body, or anything else.**
- **No third font.** Space Grotesk (in old Tailwind config) is killed.
- **Type scale (mobile)**: `text-xs (12px)` metadata · `text-sm (14px)` body · `text-base (16px)` paragraph · `text-lg (18px)` card titles · `text-xl (20px)` section headings · `text-4xl (36px)` stat numbers · `text-5xl` wordmark
- **Bold by default** for UI text. `font-extrabold` for card titles and stat numbers — the dimensional aesthetic looks weak with thin weights.

### Elevation (shadow recipes)

Three-tier scale plus specialty shadows. Each combines an inset highlight + multiple drop shadows.

```css
/* shadow-soft-1 — subtle resting elevation (chips inactive, light cards) */
box-shadow:
  inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.9),
  0 1px 2px rgba(15,15,15,0.05),
  0 4px 12px rgba(15,15,15,0.05),
  0 8px 24px rgba(15,15,15,0.04);

/* shadow-soft-2 — primary elevation (cards) */
box-shadow:
  inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.95),
  0 1px 2px rgba(15,15,15,0.06),
  0 6px 16px rgba(15,15,15,0.07),
  0 16px 32px rgba(15,15,15,0.06);

/* shadow-soft-3 — heavy elevation (modals, floating dock) */
box-shadow:
  inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.95),
  0 1px 2px rgba(15,15,15,0.08),
  0 8px 20px rgba(15,15,15,0.10),
  0 24px 48px rgba(15,15,15,0.08);

/* CTA — primary purple action */
box-shadow:
  inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.4),
  inset 0 -1px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.12),
  0 1px 2px rgba(80,20,160,0.20),
  0 6px 14px rgba(147,51,234,0.40),
  0 12px 28px rgba(147,51,234,0.22);
```

### Border radius

- Pills (status, mini): `14px` (chunky, not full-pill)
- Chips (filter, large): `999px` (full-pill — distinguishes from status pills)
- Cards: `24px` (rounded-3xl) — bigger than typical, central to the soft-3D look
- Buttons: `999px` (full-pill)
- Date chips: `14px`
- Floating dock: `28px` outer, `16px` per tab pillow

### Press feedback

```css
.press:active { transform: scale(0.985) translateY(1px); }
.press-sm:active { transform: scale(0.94); }
```

Transition: `160ms cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)` for both transform and shadow.

### Card structure

`vertical gradient white→stone-50` + `inset 1px white highlight` + `inset 0 0 0 1px hairline border at 3% opacity` + soft-2 shadow + 24px radius.

## Feature Strategy

### Keep + invest

| Feature | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Aggregation across Luma / Eventbrite / Meetup / FB | The product. Survey confirms this is the dominant pain point. |
| Editorial whitelist + review queue | The moat. Encodes operator taste; survives operator absence for weeks. |
| Filter chips: All · This week · Free · Beginner · Hiring · Online | ~13/26 survey respondents asked for filtering. Cheap to maintain. |
| Calendar subscription (iCal feed) | 2nd most-requested survey feature. Highest-leverage re-engagement: subscribe once, value forever, zero ongoing cost. |
| Saved events (localStorage, no account) | ~13/26 survey requests. Lurker-friendly. No login friction. |
| Bottom tab dock: Events / Calendar / Saved | Adopted from current Cebby. Three jobs, three tabs. |
| Map view (toggle on Events tab) | Multiple survey mentions. `EventMap.astro` already exists. |
| Explicit follow-tags (replacing "personalized recommendations") | Top-survey-feature without ML. User picks tags they care about; feed pre-filters. localStorage. |
| Weekly email digest | Re-engagement loop. Sponsor-able slot for future community-funding. |
| Cebby Wrapped (annual ritual) | Brand affinity, low ongoing cost. |

### Dormant — don't delete, don't develop

| Feature | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Auth (Logto) | Only needed for Wrapped today. Saved events do not require login. |
| Profile management | If accounts are optional, profile is a tiny menu, not a page. |
| Online users / live presence | Cute organizer-vanity. Lurkers don't care. Maintenance > zero. Hide the surface. |
| Check-in flows | Pure organizer toolkit. No lurker value. Code stays in repo for future activation. |

### Kill or never build

| Cut | Why |
|---|---|
| RSVP-on-Cebby | Maintenance burden too high, organizers want RSVPs in their own tool, no feedback loop now that the operator isn't organizing. Replaced with one-tap deep-link: "RSVP on Luma →". |
| RSVP confirmation / cancellation / day-of-reminder / waitlist email templates | All assumed Cebby owned the registration. Defunct. |
| ML-personalization based on behavior | Cebu's volume too small to learn from. Replaced with explicit follow-tags. |
| Premium tier / paywall / account upsell | Survey: most respondents prefer free. Public-utility positioning excludes this. |
| Native ads in feed | Corrupts the directory. Sponsorship belongs in clearly-labeled banner / digest slots only. |
| Speaker profiles, post-event recaps, NFT certificates, gamified referrals, leaderboards | Long-tail survey requests that don't change the core "stop platform-hopping" job. Defer indefinitely. |
| Infinite ambient animations (`glitch`, `glitchTop`, `glitchBottom`, `sparkle`, `shimmer`, `bounceIn`, `popIn`, `zoomInOut`, `gradientBG`, `floatShape`) | Fight the calm. Tax the device. Survey-validated anti-pattern. |
| Neon palette (`neon-purple`, `neon-blue`, `neon-pink`), Spotify color tokens | Vestigial from a different product direction. |
| Space Grotesk | Two fonts is a maintenance tax for no UX gain. |
| `bg-gradient.png` background | Replaced by CSS multi-radial gradient. |

## Accessibility Baseline

- **Target: WCAG AA.** Color contrast 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components.
- **Tap targets ≥ 44×44 px.** Status pills and date chips are smaller visually but always live within tappable parent surfaces (cards), never as standalone tap targets.
- **Reduced motion**: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — disable press-feedback transforms, keep static state.
- **Keyboard nav**: every interactive element must have a visible focus state (purple outline, 2px offset).
- **Screen-reader copy**: every status pill needs an accessible label (`aria-label="Free event"` not just "Free"). Source attribution must be announced.

## Survey-Validated Insights (Oct/Nov 2024, n=26)

- **22 Yes / 2 Maybe / 2 No** on aggregator interest. Cebby solves a real, validated problem.
- **Top pain points**: "too many platforms" (8+), "hard to find matching events" (8+), "info not up-to-date" (5+), "limited event details" (5+).
- **Top requested features**: personalized recommendations (16+), notifications (14+), calendar integration (13+), bookmark (13+), filtering (13+), RSVP-through-platform (13+).
- **Mode preference**: 11 in-person only, 1 virtual only, 6 depends, 5 both. Strong in-person bias.
- **Premium willingness**: 11 yes / 7 maybe / 5 no. Mixed; not a strong signal toward monetization. Reinforces public-utility framing.
- **Critical anti-pattern**: Luma's heavy 3D animations are explicitly cited as a pain point. Soft-3D must stay performant.
